Digital Table Project

Physical / digital build

Interactive table concept — expand for details.

Overview video

Problem

I play tabletop role-playing games (TTRPG), like Dungeons & Dragons, with friends. The problem with typical dungeon tiles and maps is their limited variety. If I wanted to make a snow map, the physical terrain pieces would take several days to create and would only be relevant for encounters featuring snow.

Need

TTRPGs require unique battle-maps that can adapt to a variety of unique scenarios such as deserts, sewers, mountains, and more.

Solution

A digital table constructed with a horizontal TV screen can project virtual maps to a physical space. Digital maps can be made in a fraction of the time or can be bought and downloaded from online creators.

Limitations

The screen takes up most of the table space, leaving little room for people's elbows or materials needed to play the intended game (dice, character sheets, laptops), especially with 5 to 8 players. Turning the TV on requires crawling under the table and reaching up through a 3" hole. Materials and weight are loaded on top of table legs originally designed for less weight.


Improvements

The next iteration would work better on a rectangular surface with a larger screen. The enclosure also does not need to be completely sealed, and would require and benefit from fewer materials.
